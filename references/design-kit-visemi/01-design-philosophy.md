# 01 — Design Philosophy, Theme & Style

This is the *feel* you are aiming for. It is drawn from two sources that already
agree with each other: the **live visemi.org website** and the **Cất Cánh pitch &
webinar decks**. Match both.

---

## The one-line brief

> **Credible tech polish, mission-driven warmth.** Intel/Stripe-grade restraint —
> clean grids, lots of white space, confident navy, sparing green energy — applied to
> a hopeful, human story about Vietnamese students taking off into global careers.

If a piece feels corporate-cold, add warmth (a real student photo, the rocket motif,
a human verb). If it feels busy or amateur, strip back to the grid and the palette.

---

## Five style principles (from the website)

1. **Light by default, dark for impact.** Most surfaces are white / very light gray.
   Reserve the **dark navy→purple gradient** for "feature" moments — hero banners,
   a single stat band, a closing CTA. The contrast *is* the design. (On the site
   this is `.section-tech` light vs `.section-tech--dark`.)

2. **Structured, generous grids.** 3-column card rows, clear margins, vertical
   rhythm. Never crowd. The pitch deck's numbered `01 / 02 / 03` columns are the
   signature layout — reuse it for "what we are / what we do / why."

3. **Green is energy, not wallpaper.** Green (`#01B68B`) marks momentum: CTAs,
   active states, the "Bridge/solution" side, key stat figures, accent rules.
   Navy carries the authority and most of the structure. Don't flood a layout in
   green; place it where you want the eye to go.

4. **One idea per surface.** A slide, a post, a card = one message. Big headline,
   short support line, one visual or stat. Let white space do the work.

5. **Subtle motion, never gimmick** (for any animated/video export). Gentle
   fade-and-rise reveals, a soft glow behind a hero headline, numbers that count up.
   Nothing spins, bounces, or distracts. (Mirrors the site's `fade-up` reveals,
   hero halo, and count-ups.)

### Signature surface treatments (borrow from the site)
- **Dark feature section:** navy→purple diagonal gradient with a faint **tech-grid
  overlay** (thin lines, ~3% white, fading at the edges) and a thin **cyan→green
  accent stripe** at the bottom edge. Use for heroes and closing CTAs.
- **Hero headline halo:** a soft radial green/cyan glow blurred behind the H1 on
  dark backgrounds — adds depth without clutter.
- **Cards:** white, rounded ~12px corners, hairline border, soft shadow; they "lift"
  on emphasis. On dark sections, use a translucent frosted-glass card instead.
- **Eyebrow label:** small UPPERCASE, letter-spaced, **green** kicker above a
  headline (e.g. `FELLOWSHIP` / `CHƯƠNG TRÌNH`). A fast way to add brand polish.

## The Cất Cánh program signatures

Cất Cánh is **not a separate brand** — it is the VISEMI master brand (palette, fonts,
logo) expressed through three recurring devices. Use them so any Cất Cánh piece is
instantly recognizable.

### 1. The "Takeoff" / rocket motif
"Cất Cánh" means *take flight / takeoff*. The visual shorthand is **ascent**:
- A **rocket** icon (already in the program iconography) for acceleration/launch.
- Upward diagonals, rising arrows, an upward trajectory line.
- Verbs of ascent in copy: *take off, launch, rise, bridge, accelerate.*
Use it as an accent — a corner icon, a section marker, a hero graphic — not as
decoration on every element.

### 2. The "Gaps → Bridge" storytelling device
The core narrative, straight from the pitch deck. Whenever a piece explains *why
Cất Cánh exists*, structure it as two facing columns:
- **The Gaps** (left, navy) — the problem: talent shortage, limited local MS/PhD
  paths, financial/language/strategy barriers. Cool, factual, navy-toned.
- **The Bridge** (right, green) — the solution: 6-month accelerator, tiered funding,
  global mentor network, alumni flywheel. Warm, active, green-toned.
- A **green arrow** points Gaps → Bridge. This left-problem / right-solution split
  with a connecting arrow is a reusable layout for slides, posts, and one-pagers.

### 3. Numbered pillars & stat bands
- **Pillars:** present the program as numbered cards — *Pillar 1 / 2 / 3* — each an
  icon + bold heading + one-line support (e.g. Intensive Preparation · Milestone
  Funding · Global Mentorship). White cards with a navy drop-shadow block behind.
- **Stat bands:** big bold figures (green or navy) with short labels, in a 2×3 or
  1×N grid, framed by thin accent rules. The "X Fellows Funded" hero-number layout
  is the template. **Numbers come from the brief, never hard-coded here.**

## Quick do / don't

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| Pair navy structure with sparing green energy | Flood layouts in green or use off-brand colors |
| Use real student/cohort photos when human warmth helps | Use generic stocky "business handshake" photos |
| Keep one message per slide/post | Cram multiple ideas onto one surface |
| Reuse the numbered-column / Gaps→Bridge / pillar layouts | Reinvent layout from scratch when a canon exists |
| Add the Takeoff motif as a tasteful accent | Plaster rockets on everything |
| Honor reduced-motion intent: keep motion gentle | Add spinning, bouncing, or attention-grabbing effects |
